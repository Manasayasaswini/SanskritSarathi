🕉️ Sanskrit Sarathi (संस्कृत सारथि)
Your AI Charioteer for Perfect Sanskrit Pronunciation
Sanskrit Sarathi is an AI-powered pedagogical platform designed to master the "Science of Sound" (Shiksha). Unlike standard language apps, it focuses on the precise mouth placements (Sthana) and effort (Prayatna) required for authentic Sanskrit chanting and conversation.

🚀 The Vision
To bridge the gap between ancient Vedic phonetics and modern AI, providing a personal "Charioteer" that listens, corrects, and guides students toward phonetic perfection.

✨ Key Features
🏁 Phase 1: The Foundation (MVP)
The Varna Matrix: An interactive chart of 50+ Sanskrit characters. Includes anatomical guides for tongue placement and real-time AI feedback.

Syllable-Level Scoring: Visual "Traffic Light" feedback (Green/Yellow/Red) for individual letters within a word.

Transliteration Engine: Seamlessly toggle between Devanagari, IAST (Romanized), and ITRANS.

Essential Shloka Library: 10 foundational verses (e.g., Shanti Mantras) with slow-motion playback for guided practice.

📈 Phase 2: The Journey (Intermediate)
Sandhi-Viccheda Tool: Automated breaking of complex, long compound words into readable components.

Progress Dashboard: A data-driven view of your "Phonetic Accuracy" and "Fluency Growth" over time.

Chanting Streaks: Gamified leaderboards to encourage daily Sadhana (practice).

🔮 Future Horizons
Deep Grammar Analysis: Contextual breakdown of word roots (Dhatus) and morphology.

Manuscript OCR: Use your camera to scan and hear the correct pronunciation of ancient manuscripts.

🛠️ Technical Stack
Frontend: React Native / Next.js

Backend: Python (FastAPI)

AI Models: * ASR: Bhashini Unified API / Fine-tuned OpenAI Whisper

Logic: Gemini 1.5 Flash (for corrective phonetic feedback)

Audio: 16kHz Mono PCM processing

📖 How it Works
Listen: Hear the "Golden Standard" recording of a Varna or Shloka.

Speak: Record your voice directly in the app.

Analyze: The AI compares your audio against the target phonemes.

Correct: Receive instant tips on tongue placement (e.g., "Touch your tongue to your teeth for this Dantya sound").
